About me

The son of Warren and Gloria Tann. I spent my adolsenscence in Tomball Texas, a town north of Houston. I have an older brother Christian who is an Biology alum of Texas Tech. He currently works downtown Houston for a commercial DNA enterprise. My younger sister Jessica is attending Tech in the hopes of becoming a nurse.

In highschool I graduated top 3% placing #16 of 476 students. I was vice president a member of honor society and the national hispanic honor society and a record holder for swim and track. I received the Tomball Rotary Club Scholarship, and W.A Brookshire scholarship. College Board recognized me for my acheivement in engineering and for being a national hispanic scholar. In college I became a leader for Campus Crusade For Christ (CRU) and a member of Engineering Honors, Freshman Leaders on Campus (FLOC), Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), the swim and waterpolo club.

Christianity plays a huge role in my life. I believe everyone has sinned and fallen short of the glory of God. The consequence of that sin is eternal separation from our creator. The good news of the gospel is that Jesus was who he said was: wholly God and wholly man who paid the debts of all our sins so that whoever believes in him may experience a perfect relationship with God in heaven. The purpose I have on Earth is two parts. 1. To live a rightous life that pleases God. 2. To let others know the good news.
